Background Guide

Description:

This crisis committee is set in Gilead, the world Margaret Atwood describes in her dystopian novel The Handmaidâ€™s Tale. The delegates will be members of Mayday, a secret organization seeking to overthrow an oppressive government and re-establish the world that they knew. While all Mayday members agree that Gilead is infinitely worse than anything they have ever known, some believe the Gileadan leaders are right, to a certain extent: there was something wrong with the previous system. In this committee, the delegates will have to come up with creative solutions to deal with oppression and fear, but also discover novel, concrete ways to move from dystopia to utopia.

Note: This committee is based on a book that contains mature and sensitive matters. Delegate discretion is advised.

Position Papers:

To prepare for the committee, consider carefully and answer one of the following questions:

I. Is the use of violence justifiable in the context of revolutions, and if so, to what extent and towards whom?

II. Should great social and political change be instigated by elites and flow from top to bottom, or should it involve the masses overthrowing the top?

Instead of submitting a conventional position paper, delegates are expected to submit their answer in one double-spaced page maximum.

Rebel Description:

A vast schism has formed on the Italian peninsula.The year is 73 BC and the rebellion is growing. The army, once a mere 78-men strong, has now increased to over 120,000 escaped slaves and gladiators fighting for freedom against the Roman Republic. After decades of oppression, the tensions are as high as the stakes. Fighting to end of the institution of slavery, this army, led by none other than Spartacus himself, rises up to the defy the mighty Roman legions.

In this committee delegates will be tasked with building up their armies, crafting military strategy, and definitely freeing themselves from their Roman masters for good. Meanwhile, internal disputes and a volatile morale amongst members of the Rebel Army will threaten their chance of success and even survival. Delegates, it is up to us to free ourselves from the yoke of slavery and the tyranny of our masters, and defeat the great Roman Army once and for all!

Please note: a position paper is required for this committee (either one for Rebel or one for Roman)

First Scottish War of Independence - Joint Crisis

Background Guide - England
Background Guide - Scotland

Description:

In the late 13th century King Edward I of England was looking to extend his power over the entire island of Great Britain. Having conquered the country of Wales in 1282 C.E. Edward Longshanks turned his eyes to the North and the troubled Kingdom of Scotland. The weak John I of Scotland failed at the Battle of Dunbar losing not just his throne, but the freedom of all Scots. Divided among 13 Nobleman with selfish interests, and clans who have long suffered under a feudal system, can the once proud Kingdom of Scotland mount an insurrection against the great power of England? At the same time, can England, so burdened with its wars in France, the crusades, and internal unrest, finally subdue the Northern highlands?

This Joint-Crisis Committee will be unlike any delegates have experienced. It will pit two different government structures against each other. On the one side, the English committee will be organized much like a cabinet with positions representing specific portfolios within the English kingdom. Across the border the Scots can expect a great mix of different clansmen, each weak by themselves, and strong together. The fate of Great Britain will lie in the hands of SSUNS 2016!

Please note: a position paper is required for this committee (either one for England or one for Scotland)
