Dear Prospective Staff,

Thank you for your interest in joining Secondary Schools’ United Nations Symposium 2015! Whether you are a devout Model United Nations participant or you are looking to join our conference for the first time, staffing at SSUNS promises to be an engaging, exciting and rewarding experience. Entering its 23rd year, SSUNS provides the opportunity for high school students to delve into the core workings of the United Nations by simulating committee sessions from our wide committee roster, creating thrilling, intense and challenging debate.

What makes SSUNS so successful is motivated individuals, who work diligently behind the scenes to create an exceptional conference. SSUNS welcomes staffers with a variety of interests and experiences to join our team. For those looking for leadership roles, our extensive coordinator roles are an excellent way to work closely with secretariat and staffers alike to manage your own area for the conference. For roles that are less of a time commitment but equally integral to the conference, there are a variety of staffing positions to help out in all areas of SSUNS!

If you wish to join the SSUNS team in any such capacity, please fill out our application form. If you have any questions or inquiries, please do not hesitate to contact me at staff@ssuns.org. I look forward to working closely with you and over a hundred other staff members to make SSUNS 2015 the best conference yet!

Sincerely,

Iman Lahouaoula

Coordinator Positions:

Page Coordinator:

The Page Coordinators are responsible for handling and communicating with all the pages leading up to and throughout the conference.

Delegate Resource Center Coordinator:

The Delegate Resource Center Coordinators are responsible for the delegate resource centre during SSUNS. This includes selling merchandise, providing information to SSUNS attendees and maintaining the room.

Staff Room Coordinator:

The Staff Room Coordinators are responsible for the staff room during SSUNS. This includes all equipment assigned to committees, printing resolutions for committees as well as maintaining the hub for all staff.

Events Coordinator:

The Events Coordinators are responsible for all the events leading up to and during the conference. Evens they are responsible for include staff hype night, staff bar night, the delegate dance as well as staff appreciation night.

Food Coordinator:

The Food Coordinators will be responsible for arranging all the food and snacks given to staff members, secretariat and coordinators during SSUNS. They will need to speak with local Montreal restaurants to negotiate deals. They will also need to organize and pick up the food, hence a valid driver’s license in Montreal is preferred.

Operations Coordinator:

The Operations Coordinators are responsible for the operations throughout the conference, generally relating to anything and everything technical and logistical.

Opening and Closing Ceremony Coordinator:

The Opening and Closing Ceremony coordinators are responsible for organizing and preparing all necessary materials as well as controlling the flow of opening and closing ceremonies. On the Friday and Saturday of SSUNS, these coordinators will work as “floaters” assisting in a variety of areas during the conference.

Photography Coordinator:

The photography coordinator is responsible for all official pictures of events leading up to and throughout SSUNS. They are responsible for taking photos and editing them for official use for SSUNS.

Videography Coordinator:

The Videographer coordinator is responsible for all videos taken before and during SSUNS. These include short hype videos (pre-conference) as well as opening and closing ceremony videos. They are responsible for coming up with creative footage and compiling all of it for the official use for SSUNS.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can apply?

Anyone who currently studies at McGill University; students from all majors and years are welcome to apply.

Can I be a coordinator and a committee/logistical staffer?

Due to the responsibilities of each position, you cannot be both a coordinator and a regular staffer.

What is the main difference between a coordinator or a committee/logistical staffer?

The main difference between coordinators and regular staffers is the time and work commitment.
Coordinators are assigned to a specific organizational responsibility, carried out prior to and during the conference. Thus, coordinators must be willing to commit more of their personal time to SSUNS.
Staffers assist dais members and coordinators throughout the weekend of the conference. Otherwise, there is minimal effort required on their end for pre-conference preparation.
