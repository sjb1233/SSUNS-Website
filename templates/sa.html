8th Congress United States, 1803

Background Guides

Description:

The 8th U.S. Congress is a committee that will bring you back to the frontier, facing out upon the great Mississippi and the Louisiana Territory. The year is 1803, and The United States “are” expanding westwards, currently controlled by the French, ruled by Napoleon. Meanwhile, in the heart of Washington, Congress is divided over what action might be taken in response to the assertion of judiciary power issued the Supreme Court. Further east, in the Mediterranean, U.S. Navy vessels of war are on a mission to wrest the coast of North Africa and to put an end to the malfeasant Piracy of the Barbary States. The United States is at a time of formation, growing from infant republic to baby empire – and it is up to delegates to ensure that the country continues on this path.

Canadian Confederation - Bilingual Committee

Background Guide

Description:

The Canadian Confederation committee will reenact the important debates that took place in Charlottetown and Québec City in 1864. These conferences laid the groundwork for what ultimately became the Canadian federation in 1867 by an Act of the Imperial Parliament, styled the British North America Act. Our committee begins on September 1st, 1864, the first day of the Charlottetown Conference, so as to allow our delegates to take into consideration the many options offered to them in creating this new country: a maritime union, a larger federal union, or a unitary state (what John A. Macdonald called a “legislative union”). In doing so, delegates will have to consider the impact of this union on British North America’s political institutions, its economy and its foreign policy and defense.

En tant que politiciens des colonies de l’Amérique du Nord britannique, les délégué(e)s devront affronter leurs collègues dans le but de protéger les intérêts de leur région, tout en assurant de compromettre sur les enjeux d’intérêts communs. C’est cet équilibre qui fera notamment l’objet de débats intenses durant la Conférence SSUNS 2016.

International Atomic Energy Agency 2017

Background Guide

Description:

The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) is an international organization created under the auspices of the United Nations that seeks to promote the safe, secure, and peaceful use of nuclear technologies. Headquartered in Vienna, the creation of the IAEA was first proposed by the President of the United States, Dwight D. Eisenhower in 1953 as a part of his address Atoms for Peace, to the United Nations General Assembly.

The IAEA’s Mission Statement is the following:

"The International Atomic Energy Agency:

- is an independent intergovernmental, science and technology-based organization, in the United Nations family, that serves as the global focal point for nuclear cooperation;
- assists its Member States, in the context of social and economic goals, in planning for and using nuclear science and technology for various peaceful purposes, including the generation of electricity, and facilitates the transfer of such technology and knowledge in a sustainable manner to developing Member States;
- develops nuclear safety standards and, based on these standards, promotes the achievement and maintenance of high levels of safety in applications of nuclear energy, as well as the protection of human health and the environment against ionizing radiation;
- verifies through its inspection system that States comply with their commitments, under the Non-Proliferation Treaty and other non-proliferation agreements, to use nuclear material and facilities only for peaceful purposes."

(The International Atomic Agency)

The topics that we will be tackling in the committee is the following:
Topic A: Addressing the Threat of Nuclear Terrorism
Topic B: Nuclear Energy as an Alternate Source of Energy
Topic C: Measures to Implement and Enforce the Nuclear Program in Iran

Czechoslovakia 1990

Background Guides

Description:

In 1990 a wave of change was spreading throughout the world – the fall of communism. Leaders across the world were suddenly faced with the largest change in world order since the Second World War. Delegates in the Czechoslovakia 1990 committee will face this challenge head on, leading the country through the Velvet Revolution; out of communism and into a brave new world. Furthermore, divisions are forming between the Slovaks and Czechs - who have been unified since 1918. Delegates will have the chance to discuss these issues – and many more – as they take the helm of a fractured country. In this time of upheaval anything can happen.

United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs (UNOOSA) and International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) Joint Session Committee

Background Guides

Description:

Aiming to enhance the delegate experience by holding committee sessions in the ICAO’s Montreal Headquarters, SSUNS 2016 presents the United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs (UNOOSA) and International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) Joint Session Committee. The ICAO was established in 1947 while the UNOOSA has existed in its current format since 1992 as an office in the UN Secretariat. This year’s committee will consist of captivating debates surrounding topics such as commercial space flight, space militarization, and the environmental implications of human activity in space. A strong background in international aviation and space policy will be vital, as this committee challenges the delegates to creatively push international policies and perspectives to new frontiers in space activity.

Venezuelan National Assembly

Background Guides

Description:

Delegates will represent members of the Venezuelan National Assembly that were voted in on the elections of December 6, 2015. As the members of the highest legislative power in the country, they will be tasked with dealing with the economic and social issues that currently trouble Venezuelan society. Delegates will draft legislative solutions to these issues and discuss their implications. Although the elections marked a clear victory for the opposition in the country, the pro-Maduro side will play a key role in the debate, as members of the assembly will have to set an example of openness and democracy to pave a way for the end of political polarization in Venezuela.

Microsoft Board of Directors

Background Guides

Description:

The Microsoft Board of Directors committee hands delegates the reins of one of the world’s largest companies – one that invented the modern personal computer. Times have changed however, and the company does not dominate like it once did. It is faced with an unfamiliar world – dominated by iOS and Android devices, one in which Windows does not reign supreme. Delegates will be confronted with this brave new world and be forced to deal with it head-on, focusing on the company’s market positioning, managing its mobile division and integrating its landmark Linkedin acquisition. This committee will stretch delegates’ understanding of Model United Nations – and will look for bold, new creative thinking to make sure the Next Big Thing comes from Microsoft.
